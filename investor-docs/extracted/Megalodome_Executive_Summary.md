1 · Fund Strategy & Overview
MEGALODOME GOLF is raising $25 million of equity — across three tranches — alongside $52 million of debt to develop the world’s first fully playable indoor golf course: a real, walkable course inside four interconnected climate-controlled domes that enable 365-day play in any weather. This is not a simulator venue and not a driving range; it is genuine on-course golf, indoors.
The flagship sits on a 100-acre Oswego, Illinois site (≈30 miles west of downtown Chicago) approved unanimously by the Village of Oswego Board in September 2024. The strategy is development-to-stabilization: build a differentiated, recurring-revenue, real-estate-backed asset, stabilize operations, and return capital through refinance or sale within a five-year horizon. The structural tailwind is durable — U.S. off-course golf participation (32.9M) has overtaken on-course play for the first time in history, while cold-climate metros remain structurally underserved for real winter play.
Why This Is a Category, Not a Competitor
Topgolf, Five Iron, X-Golf and traditional dome ranges monetize entertainment or practice — none deliver a real, walkable course indoors. MEGALODOME GOLF is positioned as a category creator, protected by trademark, course-design IP, and the operational expertise to run year-round play.
2 · Capital Structure
Equity arrives at two levels: Fund and Membership units at the fund level, and a direct 12.5% common stake in MEGALODOME GOLF CHICAGO WEST INC. Fund investors hold 500 Class B Units at $20,000, a 10% preferred return, 25% of distributable profits, and additional upside through MEGALODOME GOLF USA INC.; at month 60 two liquidity options apply (convert to Class A at 25%, or buyout via refinancing). 4 · Distribution Model — The Waterfall
The Fund uses a whole-fund (European-style) waterfall. Cash flows and exit proceeds are distributed in the following order:
1. The Strategic/Outside investor holds a 12.5% common stake in MEGALODOME GOLF CHICAGO WEST INC., participating in equity value at exit, plus additional upside through MEGALODOME GOLF USA INC.
2. Fund and Membership limited partners receive return of capital.
3. Fund and Membership then receive the 10% cumulative preferred return; unpaid amounts accrue (PIK) and are settled at exit.
4. Above the preferred return, exit equity follows ownership: Fund 25%, Membership 10%, Strategic/Outside 12.5%, and MEGALODOME GOLF USA INC. (Manager) 52.5%.

5 · Aligned Incentives & Downside Protection
The 10% cumulative preferred return is the Fund/Membership protected floor; MEGALODOME GOLF USA INC. (the Manager) realizes its 52.5% equity share only after the preferred return is satisfied.
Real-estate-backed asset: a 100-acre site and four domes provide tangible collateral value, not a software promise.
Nine diversified revenue streams reduce single-line volatility; recurring memberships, leagues, and school/college contracts underpin the base.
A 12-month interest reserve and 10% construction contingency are built into uses of funds — sized to cover ramp-year debt service while occupancy stabilizes.
6 · Exit Strategy
Capital is returned within a five-year horizon through one of three mechanisms: (a) refinance of the stabilized asset after Year 4, returning equity while retaining ownership; (b) sale to a strategic golf/hospitality operator or institutional real-estate buyer at a stabilized EBITDA multiple; or (c) sale-leaseback to a triple-net REIT. The base case models a Year-5 exit at 9.0x stabilized EBITDA, net of debt.

MEGALODOME GOLF is a category-defining first project, so the team frames what is true rather than fabricating realized fund returns. Four years of pre-development work have produced unanimous municipal approval, full design documentation, and a complete financial model. SportsDome Advisory contributes a 15+ year proven school-program operating model (SportsDome Bus, Sport-Étude integration) that fills weekday non-prime hours with institutional revenue — a direct, transferable operating track record.
