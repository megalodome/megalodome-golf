1 · Asset Snapshot
2 · Development Thesis — Why This Asset, Why Now
The site sits in a dense, affluent, cold-weather metro where outdoor golf closes for 5–7 months — precisely the demand window MEGALODOME GOLF captures at premium winter pricing. Off-course participation has overtaken on-course play nationally, and there is no competing product that lets a Chicago golfer play a real course in January. Municipal approval is already secured, design documentation is complete, and the going-in basis is anchored to hard construction cost ($77.0M all-in) against a stabilized 9.0x EBITDA value of $135.1M — a development-to-stabilization spread that creates the equity.
3 · Deal Economics
4 · Business Plan (Value Creation)
Develop and open the four-dome flagship and practice facility for a Fall 2027 launch.
Ramp occupancy through Year 1, layering recurring revenue: memberships (six tiers), leagues, and SportsDome school/college contracts that fill weekday non-prime hours.
Stabilize to ~62% EBITDA margin across nine revenue streams by Year 3.
Refinance after Year 4 or sell the stabilized asset, returning investor capital plus the preferred return and each class’s equity share of the upside.

8 · Why It’s Representative
This flagship is the template for the Fund’s broader pipeline: nine facilities over five years across 27 cities, built on a multi-tenant platform (“Megalodome OS”) designed for external licensing from day one. The Chicago-West economics — premium winter pricing, recurring institutional revenue, and a development-to-stabilization value spread — are the model the platform replicates. Concreteness here is intended to make the strategy tangible; it is not a representation that returns are assured.
