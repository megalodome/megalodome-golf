MEGALODOME GOLF
Investor FAQ & Objection-Handling Guide
Chicago West · MEGALODOME GOLF Equity Fund I · Reg D 506(c). Crisp, honest answers to the questions investors actually ask. Pair with the meeting choreography in the Engagement Playbook. All figures are modeled estimates, not guarantees.
The Opportunity & Category
Q.  Isn’t this just an indoor driving range or a simulator venue?
No. It is a real, walkable executive golf course on first-quality artificial turf inside four climate-controlled domes — a 9-hole par-30 you actually play, plus a separate practice facility with technology. Simulators are screens; this is the game. That distinction is the entire category.
Q.  Who is the competition?
No one offers real indoor course play — Topgolf, Five Iron and X-Golf monetize entertainment or practice, not a round of golf. We are positioned as a category creator, protected by trademark and course-design IP rather than a head-to-head feature race.
Q.  Why will you win, and stay won?
Municipal approval is secured, the design documentation is complete, and the first-mover plus IP position is hard to copy quickly. The honest risk is first-of-category execution — which is why the team, approvals and a complete model matter as much as the idea.
The Market & Demand
Q.  Is the demand actually there?
U.S. off-course golf participation (32.9M) now exceeds on-course play for the first time in history, and Chicago’s outdoor season closes for five to seven months. The model captures that closed-season window at premium winter pricing — peak winter occupancy is modeled near 90%.
Q.  Why Oswego / Chicago West?
A dense, affluent, cold-weather metro about 30 miles west of downtown Chicago, on a 100-acre site with unanimous Village of Oswego approval (Sept 2024). The siting in other materials may be shown as representative; this flagship is the intended first build.
The Numbers
Q.  How realistic is a 19.7% blended IRR / 2.35x?
It is a modeled base case: a Year-5 exit at 9.0x stabilized EBITDA (~$15.0M), net of debt, with a Year-1 ramp at ~38% realization. It is stress-tested — at a 7.5x exit the blended multiple is ~1.93x. These are targets dependent on the stated assumptions, not guarantees.
Q.  Why is Year-1 DSCR below 1.0x?
The restructured stack carries more debt ($52M), so first-year debt service (~$5.7M) modestly exceeds ramp-year EBITDA (~$5.0M) — a 0.88x coverage. That gap is pre-funded by a 12-month interest reserve sized into the uses of funds; coverage clears 1.4x from FY2028. We disclose this rather than bury it.
Q.  Why a 9.0x exit multiple?
9.0x sits at the optimistic end of golf-sector comps, so we model it as the base case and show 7.0x–9.5x in sensitivity. The hard-asset collateral — 100 acres and four domes — underpins value even if the multiple compresses.
The Structure — What You Own
Q.  What exactly would I be buying?
Three equity routes: Fund (Class B preferred) units and founding-member equity, which together form a $20M investor pool sharing a 10% preferred return; or a direct $5M outside stake for 12.5% of MEGALODOME GOLF CHICAGO WEST INC. The Fund holds 500 Class B Units at $20,000, a 10% preferred return, and 25% of distributable profits; MEGALODOME GOLF USA INC. (the Manager) holds 52.5%. (Final terms per the amended offering documents.)
Q.  How is the equity split?
After a 10% preferred return to the Fund and Membership, exit equity follows ownership: Fund 25%, Membership 10%, Strategic/Outside 12.5%, and MEGALODOME GOLF USA INC. (Manager) 52.5%. Fund and Strategic investors also participate in MEGALODOME GOLF USA INC. as additional upside.
Q.  What is my preferred return and when do distributions start?
10% per annum, cumulative and non-compounded, on the Fund + Membership pool. It accrues during construction (settled later) and begins paying in cash from FY2028 as the asset ramps.
Q.  When and how do I get my capital back?
Within a five-year horizon, through refinance after Year 4 or a sale/sale-leaseback at stabilization. Capital is locked for 60 months, at which point two liquidity options apply — convert Fund units to Class A (retaining 25%) or a Company buyout via refinancing; this is an illiquid, long-hold private investment.
Risk
Q.  What if occupancy lags the model?
Year-1 is stress-tested at 35% / 50% / 70% realization, and the interest reserve covers ramp-year service. Year-1 realization affects ramp-year cash but not the stabilized Year-5 EBITDA that drives exit value.
Q.  What if construction runs over?
A 10% construction contingency is built into the uses of funds, design documentation is complete, and Alain Desrochers (CDO) brings 30 years of process-driven development. Cost risk is real and disclosed; the contingency is the first buffer.
Q.  What if you can’t refinance or sell at exit?
Exit has three paths — refinance, strategic sale, or sale-leaseback to a triple-net REIT — and the hard real-estate asset provides collateral value independent of any single buyer. Timing and multiple are the key variables, both shown in sensitivity.
